🤔 [What Is the Google Local Pack? – The Simple Definition]

The Google Local Pack (also called the "Local 3‑Pack") is the set of three local business listings that appear at the top of Google search results for location‑based queries. It appears as a map with three businesses displayed beneath it, complete with their:

  • Business name – clickable to the website or Google Business Profile

  • Number of reviews – social proof at a glance
  • Phone number – one‑tap calling on mobile
  • Address – with directions on Google Maps
  • Hours of operation – open now or closed?
  • Photos – images of your business, products, or services
  • Website link – direct access to your site

🧩 [The 3 Key Pillars of Local Pack Ranking – Proximity, Relevance, Prominence]

Imagine this: You're applying for a job. Three things matter: your qualifications (relevance), your reputation (prominence), and your location (proximity). Google's Local Pack algorithm works exactly the same way. These three pillars determine who gets those coveted 3 spots.

📍

Pillar 1: Proximity – How Close Are You to the Searcher?

What it is: Proximity measures how close your business is to the person searching. This is calculated based on the searcher's location and your business address.

Why it matters: Google wants to show the most convenient options. If you're closer to the searcher, you have a significant advantage.

How to optimise:

  • Ensure your Google Business Profile address is 100% accurate
  • Add service areas if you serve multiple locations (e.g., Westlands, Kilimani, Karen)
  • Create location‑specific landing pages for each area you serve
  • Include local landmarks and neighbourhood names in your content

Kenyan example: A restaurant in Kilimani will rank higher for a search from Kilimani than a restaurant in Karen – even if the Karen restaurant has better reviews. Proximity is a non‑negotiable factor.

🎯

Pillar 2: Relevance – How Well Does Your Business Match the Search?

What it is: Relevance measures how well your business matches what the person is searching for. This includes your business category, keywords, products, and services.

Why it matters: Google wants to show the most relevant results. If your business is clearly about what they're searching for, you'll rank higher.

How to optimise:

  • Choose the most accurate primary category for your business
  • Add secondary categories that describe your services
  • Include keywords in your Google Business Profile description
  • List all your products and services on your profile
  • Create location‑specific content on your website
  • Use local keywords in your meta tags and headings

Kenyan example: A restaurant that has "restaurant" as its primary category and "nyama choma," "Kenyan cuisine," "family restaurant" as secondary categories will rank better for those searches. Relevance drives visibility.

🏆

Pillar 3: Prominence – How Well‑Known and Trusted Is Your Business?

What it is: Prominence measures how well‑known, trusted, and authoritative your business is. This includes reviews, ratings, citations, and backlinks.

Why it matters: Google wants to show businesses that are trusted by the community. More reviews, higher ratings, and more citations = higher prominence.

How to optimise:

  • Earn 50+ 5‑star reviews on Google Business Profile
  • Build 20+ local citations (directories, Yellow Pages, Pigiame)
  • Earn backlinks from local Kenyan blogs and media
  • Respond to all reviews – positive and negative
  • Post regularly on your Google Business Profile
  • Get featured in local news and community sites

Kenyan example: A restaurant with 100+ reviews and a 4.9★ rating will consistently outrank a restaurant with 10 reviews and a 4.0★ rating – even if they're equally relevant and close. Prominence is the differentiator.

Key insight: All three pillars work together. You need proximity (you're close), relevance (you match the search), and prominence (you're trusted). Optimise all three to dominate the Local Pack.
