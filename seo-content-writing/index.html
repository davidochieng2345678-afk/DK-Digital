🎓 Expertise – Formal Knowledge & Skills

  • CTA: What action should the reader take?

10 Common Content Writing Mistakes – And How to Fix Them

Even well‑intentioned content can fail. These 10 mistakes are the most common reasons Kenyan businesses don't see results from their content – and exactly how to fix them to start ranking and converting.

⚠️ Why These Mistakes Cost Kenyan Businesses Thousands in Lost Revenue

Every mistake on this list is one we've seen firsthand – from Nairobi startups to established Kenyan brands. These errors are why businesses spend money on content but see zero ROI. Here's why they matter:

Poor Rankings

1

📄 Mistake #1: Writing Thin, Shallow Content

The most common mistake – and the easiest to fix
❌ The Problem
  • Blog posts under 300 words
  • Product descriptions with 1‑2 sentences
  • Service pages that just list features
  • No explanations, examples, or depth
✅ The Fix
  • Blog posts: 1,500‑2,500+ words
  • Product descriptions: 200‑300+ words with benefits
  • Service pages: 1,000‑1,500+ words with case studies
  • Add examples, data, and user testimonials

💡 Kenya example: "We sell Ankara dresses" → "Discover our collection of premium Ankara dresses, crafted by Kenyan designers using high‑quality African prints. Perfect for weddings, church, and special occasions – with free delivery in Nairobi."

2

🔑 Mistake #2: Keyword Stuffing – Forcing Keywords Unnaturally

Google penalises this – and readers hate it
❌ The Problem
  • Repeating the same keyword 20+ times
  • Keyword density > 5%
  • Forcing keywords where they don't belong
  • Content sounds robotic and unnatural
✅ The Fix
  • Use keywords naturally – write for humans first
  • Keyword density 1‑2%
  • Use synonyms and related terms
  • Include keywords in H1, title, and first 100 words

💡 Kenya example: "best plumber Nairobi, best plumber Nairobi, best plumber Nairobi" → "Looking for a reliable plumber in Nairobi? Our team of licensed professionals has been serving homes across the city since 2015 – from emergency repairs to full bathroom installations."

3

🎯 Mistake #3: Ignoring User Intent – Writing Content That Doesn't Match What Searchers Want

This is why people bounce within seconds
❌ The Problem
  • Writing about "how to" when user wants to buy
  • Promotional content when user wants information
  • Not answering the question being asked
  • No clear next step or CTA
✅ The Fix
  • Identify search intent before writing
  • Match content format to intent (blog, product, landing)
  • Answer the question immediately
  • Include a clear CTA for commercial intent

💡 Kenya example: Someone searches "best wireless headphones Kenya" – they want to BUY. Don't write a 2,000‑word history of headphones. Give them top 10 recommendations with prices, availability, and a clear CTA to buy.

4

🇰🇪 Mistake #4: No Local SEO – Content That Could Be Anywhere in the World

This is the biggest missed opportunity for Kenyan businesses
❌ The Problem
  • No mention of "Kenya," "Nairobi," or location
  • No reference to KES – all prices in USD
  • No local examples or case studies
  • No citations to Kenyan authorities
✅ The Fix
  • Include "Nairobi," "Kenya," "KES" naturally
  • Use local examples from Kenyan businesses
  • Reference Kenyan authorities (KRA, KNBS, CBK)
  • Include local pricing in KES

💡 Kenya example: "Our cleaning services are affordable" → "Our Nairobi cleaning services start from KES 2,500 – trusted by 200+ homes in Westlands, Kilimani, and Kileleshwa."

5

📋 Mistake #5: Poor Headings & Structure – Walls of Text Kill Engagement

If it's not scannable, it won't be read
❌ The Problem
  • No headings – just one block of text
  • Headings that don't describe content
  • No subheadings to break up content
  • No bullet points or lists
✅ The Fix
  • Use H1, H2, H3, H4 hierarchy
  • Headings should be descriptive and include keywords
  • Break content into short paragraphs (2‑3 sentences)
  • Use bullet points and lists for scannability

💡 Kenya example: A blog post about "Cleaning Tips for Nairobi Homes" should have H2s like: "Tip 1: Daily Cleaning Routine," "Tip 2: Deep Cleaning Your Kitchen," "Tip 3: Natural Cleaning Products in Kenya" – not just one long paragraph.

6

📢 Mistake #6: No Call‑to‑Action – Writing Content That Goes Nowhere

Content without a CTA is content without results
❌ The Problem
  • No CTA at the end of blog posts
  • Generic CTAs like "Contact us" (weak)
  • CTA doesn't match the content
  • CTA buried and hard to find
✅ The Fix
  • Include a clear CTA in every piece
  • Make it actionable – "Book a Free Consultation"
  • Match CTA to content intent (blog → learn more, product → buy)
  • Place CTA above the fold and at the end

💡 Kenya example: Instead of "Contact us," try: "Ready to transform your Nairobi home? Book a free cleaning quote – we'll come to you within 24 hours. Click here to get started."

7

🔗 Mistake #7: No Internal Links – Isolated Content That Doesn't Build Authority

Internal linking is one of the most underutilised SEO tools
❌ The Problem
  • No links to other pages on your site
  • Content exists in isolation
  • No distribution of page authority
  • Users don't find other relevant content
✅ The Fix
  • Add 3‑5 internal links per blog post
  • Link to related products, services, or posts
  • Use descriptive anchor text
  • Create a pillar content strategy

💡 Kenya example: In a blog post about "Ankara styling tips," link to your "Ankara dress collection" product page and your "Nairobi fashion trends" blog post.

8

🖼️ Mistake #8: No Images or Visuals – Boring, Unengaging Content

Visual content gets 94% more views than text‑only
❌ The Problem
  • No images in blog posts
  • Poor quality or irrelevant images
  • No alt text on images
  • Images not optimised (slow loading)
✅ The Fix
  • Add 1‑2 images per 500 words
  • Use high‑quality, relevant images
  • Add descriptive alt text with keywords
  • Compress images for fast loading

💡 Kenya example: For a blog post about "Nairobi restaurants," include photos of the restaurants, signature dishes, and interior – with alt text like "best restaurant in Westlands Nairobi interior."

9

🧠 Mistake #9: No Author Bio or E‑E‑A‑T Signals – Content With No Credibility

Google needs to know who wrote your content
❌ The Problem
  • No author name on blog posts
  • No author bio with credentials
  • No author photo
  • No links to LinkedIn or professional profile
✅ The Fix
  • Add author name and bio to every post
  • Include credentials and experience
  • Add author photo
  • Link to LinkedIn or professional profile

💡 Kenya example: "By David Ochieng Kiruo – SEO Expert with 8+ years of experience helping 100+ Kenyan businesses rank on Google. Connect on LinkedIn." – with photo.

10

🔄 Mistake #10: No Updates – Content That's Outdated and Losing Rankings

Google rewards fresh, updated content
❌ The Problem
  • Content 2+ years old with no updates
  • Outdated statistics and information
  • Broken links and dead references
  • No "Last updated" date
✅ The Fix
  • Update content every 3‑6 months
  • Add new statistics and data
  • Fix broken links
  • Add "Last updated" date to show freshness

💡 Kenya example: A 2023 blog post about "Best wireless headphones in Kenya" – update it with 2026 models, new prices, and fresh recommendations. Add "Updated January 2026" to show Google it's current.

🧠 Expert SEO Content Recommendation for Kenyan Businesses

After writing and optimizing thousands of pages for Kenyan clients, we’ve identified three content mistakes that kill SEO:

  1. Thin content (under 300 words) – Google cannot assess value. Aim for at least 800 words for blog posts, 400+ for product descriptions.
  2. Keyword stuffing – forcing keywords unnaturally. Write for humans first, then optimize.
  3. Ignoring user intent – writing content that doesn’t match what searchers actually want.

Our recommendation: start with a free content audit, then prioritize rewriting your most important pages (homepage, top products, key service pages) with unique, valuable, optimized content. This alone can double your organic traffic within 3 months.
