⚠️ [6 Common Mistakes Kenyan Businesses Make with GBP – And How to Avoid Them]

Over the years, I've seen businesses lose out on thousands of customers because of these avoidable errors. Here are the most common GBP mistakes and how to fix them.

❌

Incomplete or Incorrect Categories

Mistake: Choosing only one category or using generic categories. Fix: Select a primary category that best describes your business, and add up to 9 secondary categories. For example, a restaurant should choose "Restaurant" as primary, and "Takeout," "Delivery," "Café," etc. as secondary.

❌

No Photos or Low-Quality Photos

Mistake: Using blurry, poorly-lit, or generic stock photos. Fix: Invest in professional photography. Show your storefront, interior, products, team, and happy customers. Update photos regularly to keep your profile fresh.

❌

Ignoring Customer Reviews

Mistake: Not responding to reviews, especially negative ones. Fix: Respond to every review within 24 hours. Thank positive reviewers, and address negative feedback professionally. This shows engagement and builds trust.

❌

Inconsistent NAP Information

Mistake: Different phone numbers or address variations across directories. Fix: Audit your NAP across 50+ directories and ensure consistency. Use tools like Moz Local or BrightLocal to automate this process.

❌

Not Using Google Posts

Mistake: Posting once in a blue moon – or never. Fix: Post at least once a week. Share offers, events, new products, or helpful tips. Google favors active profiles.

❌

Not Tracking Performance

Mistake: Setting up GBP and forgetting about it. Fix: Track your profile views, calls, direction requests, and search queries weekly. Use Google's dashboard and our monthly reports to monitor progress and adjust strategy.
