1. Why “Plumber Nairobi” Won’t Rank (And What to Target Instead)

Many Kenyan business owners assume that targeting the most obvious keyword is the best strategy. It is not. Let’s analyze why.

3. How to Find Keywords – Free Tools & Step‑by‑Step Process

You do not need expensive tools. Start with these free methods – they work for Kenyan websites.

Step 1: Mine Google Autocomplete (Instant Keyword Ideas)

Open Google Kenya (google.co.ke). Start typing a seed keyword (e.g., “best restaurant”) and note the suggestions. Then add a space and type a letter (e.g., “best restaurant a”, “best restaurant b”) to get more variations. Do this for your main services and locations.

Example seed keywords for a Nairobi plumber: “emergency plumber”, “plumber near me”, “how to fix”, “cost of plumber”. Each will generate dozens of long‑tail keyword ideas.

Step 2: Use “People Also Ask” (PAA) Boxes

Search for your seed keyword on Google. Scroll down to the “People also ask” section. Each question is a voice‑optimized keyword. Click on a question – more questions appear. Extract at least 20 PAA questions per topic.

Step 3: Analyze Competitor Keywords (Free)

Identify your top 3 competitors in Google search. Visit their websites. Look at their page titles, H1 tags, and content. What phrases do they repeat? Use a free tool like Keyworddit (Reddit keyword extractor) or simply view page source to see meta keywords.

Step 4: Use AnswerThePublic (3 Free Searches Per Day)

Go to answerthepublic.com. Enter a keyword like “SEO Nairobi”. It generates hundreds of question‑based and preposition‑based keywords (near, for, with, without). Each is a potential voice query. Use your 3 free searches wisely.

Step 5: Google Search Console – Your Own Data

If you already have Search Console installed, go to Performance → Queries. Sort by impressions. Look for phrases that are already bringing traffic – those are your existing keyword assets. Optimize those pages further. Also look for queries with high impressions but low clicks – improve your title and meta description for those.

Step 6: Add Swahili and Sheng Variations

Use Google Translate or a native speaker to translate your main keywords. Examples:

  • “Best phone repair near me” → “Ukarabati wa simu bora karibu nami”
  • “Affordable house for rent” → “Nyumba ya kukodi bei rahisi”

These have almost zero competition. Even if search volume is low, ranking for 10 Swahili phrases can bring you unique customers that competitors ignore.

4. How to Check Keyword Competition (Free Method)

You do not need a paid tool to estimate competition. Use this free method:

  1. Search for the exact keyword phrase in incognito mode on Google.
  2. Look at the top 3 results. Are they big brands, national directories (e.g., Yellow Pages, Jumia), or Wikipedia? If yes, competition is high – you may struggle to rank.
  3. If the top results are small local businesses, forums (Reddit, Quora), or blogs, competition is low to medium – you can likely outrank them with better content.
  4. Check if there is a “People also ask” box or featured snippet – those are opportunities to steal the snippet.
  5. Look at the word count and quality of the top results. If they are thin (under 500 words), you can beat them with a 1,200+ word comprehensive guide.

Target keywords where: Top results are not big brands, content is thin, and there is a featured snippet opportunity. These are your low‑hanging fruit.
