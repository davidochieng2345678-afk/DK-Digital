1
Brainstorm Seed Keywords

2
Add Location Modifiers

Add Nairobi location modifiers to your seed keywords. Include neighborhoods and landmarks that customers use to find businesses.

Locations to include: Nairobi, Westlands, Kilimani, Karen, CBD, Lavington, Kileleshwa, Parklands, Langata, South B, South C, Donholm, Buru Buru, Kasarani, Ruiru, Kiambu, Thika
3
Use Keyword Research Tools

Use tools to find search volume and competition levels. Start with free tools – they're highly effective for local keyword research.

Recommended free tools: Google Keyword Planner, Google Autocomplete, AnswerThePublic, Ubersuggest, Google Search Console
4
Analyze Competitors

Look at what keywords your competitors are ranking for. This reveals gaps and opportunities where you can outrank them.

How: Search your primary keywords in Google • Check competitor GBP • Use "People Also Ask"
5
Prioritize & Organize

Sort keywords by search volume and competition. Create a priority list with high-intent keywords at the top. Build content around these.

Priority order: High volume + low competition → High volume + high competition → Low volume + low competition

📍 Location Pages – The Cornerstone of Local SEO

Location pages are dedicated pages on your website for each area you serve. They tell Google exactly where you operate and help you rank for neighborhood-specific searches.

🏗️ Location Page Structure
  • Title Tag: Include location + service + "Nairobi"

  • Contact: Local phone number
  • Testimonials: From customers in that area
  • Schema: LocalBusiness markup

📝 Schema Markup – The Secret Weapon for Local Rankings

Schema markup is code that helps search engines understand your business better. It's the difference between ranking and dominating local search.

🏢 LocalBusiness Schema

Tells Google everything about your business – name, address, phone, hours, coordinates, price range, and more.

@type: "LocalBusiness"
name: "Your Business Name"
address: {...}
geo: {...}
⭐ Review Schema

Shows star ratings in search results. Businesses with review schema get 36% more clicks than those without.

@type: "AggregateRating"
ratingValue: "4.8"
reviewCount: "25"
🚀 FAQ Schema

Shows questions and answers directly in search results. This increases click-through rate and positions you as an authority.

@type: "FAQPage"
mainEntity: [...]
📅 Event Schema

Shows upcoming events in search results. Perfect for workshops, promotions, and open houses.

@type: "Event"
name: "Grand Opening"
startDate: "2026-..."

🔑 Pro tip: Use Google's Structured Data Testing Tool to validate your schema markup. Most businesses don't use schema – giving you a massive competitive advantage.
