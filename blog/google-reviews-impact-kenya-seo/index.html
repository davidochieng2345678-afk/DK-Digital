Step 1: Encourage Customers to Leave Reviews

The boutique created simple ways for customers to leave reviews:

  • Post-purchase emails with direct Google review links
  • QR codes on receipts directing to Google Business Profile
  • Friendly in-store reminders after great service
  • Incentives (small discounts) – within Google’s guidelines

Result: They went from 5 to 50+ reviews in 3 months.

Step 2: Respond to Every Review – Good and Bad

Responding to reviews builds trust and engagement:

  • Positive reviews: Thank the customer, use their name, and mention your business name (e.g., “Thank you for choosing [Business Name]!”).
  • Negative reviews: Apologize professionally, offer to resolve offline, and never argue.
  • Google sees response activity as a sign of an active, trustworthy business.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions – Reviews & Local SEO

How do Google reviews affect local SEO?
Google reviews are a top local ranking factor. They signal trust, relevance, and credibility – directly impacting your Local Pack position. Businesses with more positive, recent reviews outrank competitors.
How many reviews do I need to rank locally?
Quality and recency matter more than quantity. 20+ recent positive reviews can significantly boost rankings. But even 10 five‑star reviews can make a difference in less competitive niches.
Should I respond to negative reviews?
Yes. Responding professionally shows you care and can mitigate damage. Google also considers response activity as a sign of an active, trustworthy business. Never argue – offer to resolve offline.
How much does review management cost in Kenya?
Review management services (review generation, monitoring, response) start at $99/month. Free $100 audit included.
Can I ask customers for reviews?
Yes, as long as you follow Google’s guidelines. Don’t offer incentives in exchange for positive reviews specifically, but you can ask happy customers to share their experience.
What platforms besides Google should I collect reviews on?
TripAdvisor (for tourism), Facebook, Yelp, Yellow Pages Kenya, and industry‑specific directories. Cross‑platform consistency builds authority.
