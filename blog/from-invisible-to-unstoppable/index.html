Step 1: Find Your Seed Keywords

Start with your main services and expand them into search terms. For example, if you offer “SEO services,” your seed keywords might be:

  • SEO services Nairobi
  • Affordable SEO Kenya
  • Local SEO expert

Brainstorm 10‑20 seed keywords that describe your business. These are the foundation for deeper research.

Step 2: Use Free & Paid SEO Tools

Google Keyword Planner – Free – search volume, competition.
Ubersuggest – Free (limited) – keyword ideas, difficulty.
AnswerThePublic – Free – question‑based keywords.
Ahrefs / SEMrush – Paid (trial) – advanced competitor data.

👉 Top free SEO tools for Kenyan startups →

Step 3: Focus on Long‑Tail Keywords (The Secret Weapon)

Generic keywords like “SEO Kenya” are extremely competitive. Instead, target long‑tail keywords (4+ words) that have lower competition and higher conversion rates. Examples:

  • “affordable SEO services Nairobi for startups”
  • “how to rank on Google in Kenya fast”
  • “best local SEO expert for small business in Nairobi”

Long‑tail keywords account for 70% of all searches. They are easier to rank for and bring ready‑to‑buy customers.

Step 4: Competitor Keyword Analysis

Study what top‑ranking websites are doing – and do it better. Use tools like Ahrefs or SEMrush to:

  • See which keywords your competitors rank for
  • Find keyword gaps (keywords they rank for but you don’t)
  • Analyze their content structure and backlinks

Step 5: Map Keywords to a Content Strategy

  • Assign primary keywords to each page (homepage, services, blog posts)
  • Use internal linking between related pages
  • Optimize headings (H1, H2, H3) and meta tags
  • Create content clusters (pillar page + supporting articles)
