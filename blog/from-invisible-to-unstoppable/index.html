🌱 Step 1: Find Your Seed Keywords – The Foundation of Everything

Before you can research keywords, you need a starting point. Seed keywords are the core terms that describe your business – the foundation you'll expand into hundreds of long‑tail keywords. This is where every successful keyword strategy begins.

🤔 What Are Seed Keywords?

Seed keywords are the basic, core terms that define your business. They're usually 1–3 words and represent your products, services, or industry. Think of them as the roots of your keyword tree – from these roots, you'll grow dozens of branches (long‑tail keywords).

📌 Examples of seed keywords:

SEO services interior design logistics company real estate agent restaurant Nairobi lawyer Kenya ecommerce store digital marketing agency

🧠 How to Brainstorm Your Seed Keywords – 5 Simple Steps

Most business owners overthink this step. Don't. Just write down everything that describes your business. Here's a simple 5‑step process to get you started.

1 List Your Products & Services

Write down every single thing you sell or offer. Be specific. Don't just write "SEO" – write "SEO audits, keyword research, link building, content writing."

Example: Interior designer → "residential design, office design, renovation consulting, space planning, color consulting"
2 Add Your Location

Where do you operate? Add city names, neighborhoods, and regions. "Nairobi", "Westlands", "Mombasa", "Kisumu", "Kenya" – these are gold for local SEO.

Example: "Interior design Nairobi", "office design Westlands", "home renovation Mombasa"
3 Think About Your Customers

What would your ideal customer search for? If you're a lawyer, they might search for "divorce lawyer Nairobi" or "contract dispute lawyer Kenya." Put yourself in their shoes.

Example: "affordable interior designer", "best interior designer for apartments", "interior designer near me"
4 Consider Problems You Solve

What problems does your business solve? What pain points do your customers have? These become powerful keyword opportunities.

Example: "how to make a small apartment look bigger", "affordable office renovation Nairobi"
5 Check Your Competitors

What keywords are your competitors targeting? Look at their website content, blog posts, and meta tags. This is the fastest way to find keywords you haven't thought of.

Pro tip: Use the "Searches related to" section at the bottom of Google search results to find more seed keywords.

🚫 Common Mistakes When Brainstorming Seed Keywords

Avoid these 5 mistakes that Kenyan businesses make when starting their keyword research.

❌ Mistake 1: Being Too General

"I sell shoes" → "shoes" is too broad. "athletic shoes," "formal shoes," "women's shoes" – get specific.

❌ Mistake 2: Forgetting Location

Kenyan customers search locally. Always add "Nairobi," "Kenya," or specific neighborhoods to your seed keywords.

❌ Mistake 3: Ignoring Customer Language

Don't use industry jargon. Use the words your customers use – not what you use internally.

❌ Mistake 4: Brainstorming Once

Your business evolves. Your seed keywords should too. Review and update them every 3–6 months.

❌ Mistake 5: Not Thinking About Intent

Are people searching for information (how to), comparison (best), or transaction (buy, hire)? Include keywords for every stage of the customer journey.

How to fix it: Take your seed keywords and ask yourself – "Would my customer actually search for this?" If the answer is "maybe" or "no," refine it.

  • Conversion rate: 5–8%

The key insight: 10 people searching for "affordable interior designer for small apartments in Nairobi" are 10x more valuable than 100 people searching for "interior design" – because they're ready to buy.

📊 Keyword Gaps

📝 Content Strategy

🔎 Step 1: Identify Your True Competitors

Most businesses think their competitors are obvious – but they're often wrong. Here's how to find your true SEO competitors:

🔍 Method 1: Google Search

Search for your top 5 seed keywords and see who appears on page 1. These are your SEO competitors – regardless of whether you consider them "real" competitors.

Example: Search "interior design Nairobi" – note the top 5–10 websites.
💡 Method 2: Google Maps

Open Google Maps, search for your service in your location, and see who appears in the top 3. These are your local competitors – they're winning at GBP optimization.

Example: Search "interior designers near Westlands" – note the top 3 GBP listings.
🛠️ Method 3: Use Ahrefs/SEMrush

Enter your website in Ahrefs or SEMrush and use the "Competing Domains" feature. These tools will show you which websites rank for the same keywords.

Tip: You can use the free version of Ubersuggest for basic competitor data.
📋 Method 4: Ask Your Customers

Ask your existing customers: "Who else did you consider before choosing us?" This gives you a list of businesses that are actually competing for your clients.

Example: Grace asked her clients and discovered 3 other interior designers she'd never heard of.

📊 Step 2: Analyze Their Keywords – What's Working for Them

Once you've identified your competitors, it's time to see exactly what keywords they rank for. Here's the process:

📌 Step A: Enter Their URL in Ubersuggest

Ubersuggest's free version shows you top keywords for any domain. Look for keywords with high volume and positions 1–20.

📌 Step B: Note Their Top 20 Keywords

Create a spreadsheet and list every keyword your competitor ranks for. Pay special attention to keywords where they're #1–#5.

📌 Step C: Identify Your Keyword Gaps

Compare their list to yours. Which keywords are they ranking for that you're not? These are your opportunity keywords.

The goal: Find 20–30 keywords that your competitors rank for but you don't. These are your quickest wins.

📝 Step 3: Analyze Their Content – What's Working for Them

Keywords alone aren't enough. You need to understand how they structure their content and what makes it rank. Here's a checklist:

📏 Content Length

How long are their articles? 300 words or 2,000 words? If they're ranking with 2,000 words, you need to match or exceed that.

Data: Average first‑page result is 1,500+ words.
🏷️ Headings Structure

Do they use clear H1, H2, H3 structure? Are their headings keyword‑rich? This tells you how they organize their content for SEO.

Tip: Copy their heading structure and improve on it.
🔗 Internal Links

How do they link between their pages? Do they have a content hub structure? This helps Google understand their site architecture.

Data: Sites with strong internal linking rank 40% higher.
🖼️ Visual Content

Do they use images, videos, or infographics? Visual content keeps users engaged and reduces bounce rate.

Data: Posts with images get 94% more views.
📢 CTAs (Call to Action)

Do they have clear CTAs? Where are they placed? This tells you how they convert visitors into leads.

Tip: Place CTAs above the fold and at the end of each post.
⭐ Schema Markup

Do they use FAQ, Review, or Breadcrumb schema? Schema helps Google display rich snippets.

Data: Rich snippets increase CTR by 20–30%.

🔗 Step 4: Analyze Their Backlinks – Who's Linking to Them

Backlinks are one of the top 3 ranking factors. If your competitors have strong backlinks, you need to match or exceed them. Here's how:

📌 Step A: Identify Their Backlink Sources

Use Ahrefs, SEMrush, or Ubersuggest to see which sites are linking to your competitors. Look for patterns – are they getting links from Kenyan blogs, industry directories, or news sites?

📌 Step B: Analyze Link Quality

Not all backlinks are equal. Check the Domain Authority of linking sites. A link from a DA 60 site is 10x more valuable than a link from a DA 20 site.

📌 Step C: Identify Link Opportunities

Which of these linking sites could also link to you? Reach out to the same sites with better content or a unique angle.

📌 Step D: Find Their Broken Links

Use Ahrefs' "Broken Links" feature to find dead links on competitor sites. Offer your content as a replacement – this is a powerful link‑building tactic.

Pro tip: In our case study, broken link building delivered a 42% success rate – much higher than cold outreach. Always prioritize broken links.

Step 1: Find Your Seed Keywords

Start with your main services and expand them into search terms. For example, if you offer “SEO services,” your seed keywords might be:

  • SEO services Nairobi
  • Affordable SEO Kenya
  • Local SEO expert

Brainstorm 10‑20 seed keywords that describe your business. These are the foundation for deeper research.

Step 2: Use Free & Paid SEO Tools

Google Keyword Planner – Free – search volume, competition.
Ubersuggest – Free (limited) – keyword ideas, difficulty.
AnswerThePublic – Free – question‑based keywords.
Ahrefs / SEMrush – Paid (trial) – advanced competitor data.

👉 Top free SEO tools for Kenyan startups →

Step 3: Focus on Long‑Tail Keywords (The Secret Weapon)

Generic keywords like “SEO Kenya” are extremely competitive. Instead, target long‑tail keywords (4+ words) that have lower competition and higher conversion rates. Examples:

  • “affordable SEO services Nairobi for startups”
  • “how to rank on Google in Kenya fast”
  • “best local SEO expert for small business in Nairobi”

Long‑tail keywords account for 70% of all searches. They are easier to rank for and bring ready‑to‑buy customers.

Step 4: Competitor Keyword Analysis

Study what top‑ranking websites are doing – and do it better. Use tools like Ahrefs or SEMrush to:

  • See which keywords your competitors rank for
  • Find keyword gaps (keywords they rank for but you don’t)
  • Analyze their content structure and backlinks

Step 5: Map Keywords to a Content Strategy

  • Assign primary keywords to each page (homepage, services, blog posts)
  • Use internal linking between related pages
  • Optimize headings (H1, H2, H3) and meta tags
  • Create content clusters (pillar page + supporting articles)
