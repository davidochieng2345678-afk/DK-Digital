🔍 Factors That Determine SEO Cost – What Affects Your Price

Why does one business pay KES 40,000/month while another pays KES 80,000+? The answer is not that one is being overcharged – it's that the scope of work is different. In this section, we'll break down the 7 key factors that determine SEO pricing, so you understand exactly why your quote is what it is – and what you're getting for your money.

📊 Factor 1 – Competition Level

This is the #1 factor affecting SEO cost. The more competitors you have ranking for your target keywords, the more work is required to outrank them.

🟢 Low Competition
  • Niche keywords
  • Less than 5 competitors
  • Cost: KES 10,000–30,000/mo
  • Results in 3–4 months
🟡 Medium Competition
  • Industry keywords
  • 5–15 competitors
  • Cost: KES 30,000–60,000/mo
  • Results in 4–6 months
🔴 High Competition
  • Broad, high‑value keywords
  • 15+ competitors
  • Cost: KES 60,000–100,000+/mo
  • Results in 6–12 months
Example: Ranking for "SEO services Nairobi" (high competition) requires more work than "affordable SEO for salons in Westlands" (low competition). Niche keywords cost less.

🛠️ Factor 2 – Website Condition

The current state of your website significantly impacts the cost of SEO. A site with technical issues requires more work upfront than a well‑optimized site.

✅ Healthy Site
  • Fast load time (<3s)
  • Mobile‑friendly
  • Good content (800+ words per page)
  • No major technical issues
  • Cost: Lower (less fixing needed)
⚠️ Poor Site
  • Slow load time (>4s)
  • Not mobile‑friendly
  • Thin content (under 400 words)
  • Broken links, indexing issues
  • Cost: Higher (significant fixes needed)
Example: In our Local SEO Case Study, fixing technical issues (speed, mobile, indexing) took 2 months – but moved rankings up 5–8 positions.

📝 Factor 3 – Content Needs

The problem with many SEO agencies is that they quote a low monthly fee – then add on charges for essential services you assumed were included. Here's how it works:

  • "SEO Package – KES 15,000/month"

The lesson: Always ask "What's included?" in detail. If it's not listed in writing, don't assume it's included.

🔍 7 Hidden Costs to Watch Out For

Here are the 7 most common hidden costs that SEO agencies don't always tell you about:

1 One‑Time Setup Fees

Many agencies charge a one‑time setup fee for initial work – audit, GBP setup, technical fixes. This can range from KES 20,000–100,000 and is often not included in the monthly price.

💡 Ask: "Is there a one‑time setup fee? What's included?"
2 Content Writing – Per Post Charges

Some agencies limit blog posts and charge extra for each additional post. Content writing can cost KES 2,000–10,000 per post depending on quality and length.

💡 Ask: "How many blog posts are included? What's the cost per additional post?"
3 Link Building – Per Link Costs

Building high‑quality backlinks takes time and money. Some agencies charge KES 2,000–15,000 per link on top of your monthly fee – or limit the number of links included.

💡 Ask: "How many backlinks are included? What's the cost per additional link?"
4 Technical SEO – Additional Fixes

Technical fixes (site speed, mobile, indexing) are often not included in basic packages. Fixing these can cost KES 10,000–50,000 depending on the complexity.

💡 Ask: "Are technical SEO fixes included? If not, what's the cost?"
5 Tool & Software Costs

Some agencies pass on the cost of SEO tools (Ahrefs, SEMrush, Moz) to you. This can add KES 5,000–15,000/month to your bill.

💡 Ask: "Are SEO tools included in the monthly fee?"
6 Photos, Graphics, and Design

Professional photos, graphics, or video creation for your website and GBP are rarely included. These can cost KES 2,000–20,000 depending on the complexity.

💡 Ask: "Are photos and graphics included? If not, what's the cost?"
7 Contract Termination Fees

Some agencies lock you into long‑term contracts with early termination fees. This can cost KES 30,000–100,000+ if you want to leave early.

💡 Ask: "Is there a contract? What are the termination fees?"
