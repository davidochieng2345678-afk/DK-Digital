📍 The Google Local Pack – Simple Definition

The Google Local Pack (also called the "Local 3-Pack" or "Map Pack") is a featured section in Google search results that displays three local businesses related to a user's search query. It appears above organic search results and includes:

📍
Business name
Clickable link to your Google Business Profile
⭐

Shows how customers rate your business
📞

🗺️
Map location
Shows your business on an interactive map
🕐
Business hours
Current operating hours and holiday schedules
📸
Photos
Images of your business, products, and team
🎯 In Simple Terms:

When someone searches for "boutique near me Nairobi" or "best coffee shop Westlands", Google shows the top 3 local businesses in a prominent box at the top of the page. This is the Local Pack. If you're not in it, you're invisible to customers searching for businesses like yours.

🔍 How the Google Local Pack Works – The Algorithm Explained

Google's local algorithm uses three main factors to determine which businesses appear in the Local Pack. Understanding these factors is the first step to ranking:

📍
1. Relevance
How well your business matches the search query. This includes your business categories, description, and the keywords you use on your Google Business Profile and website.
Kenya example: A boutique with category "Women's clothing store" will appear for "boutique near me Nairobi" if its profile is optimised.
⭐
2. Prominence
How well-known and trusted your business is. This is measured by the quantity, quality, and recency of your reviews, as well as your overall online presence.
Kenya example: A boutique with 35+ five-star reviews will outrank one with 5 reviews and 3.5 stars.
📏
3. Distance
How close your business is to the searcher's location. Google prioritises businesses that are physically closer to the person searching.
Kenya example: A boutique in Nairobi CBD will rank higher for "boutique near me Nairobi CBD" than one in Westlands.

✅ The takeaway: To rank in the Local Pack, you need to optimise all three factors. Relevance (categories, keywords), Prominence (reviews, reputation), and Distance (your physical location). At DK Digital SEO, we optimise all three – delivering consistent Local Pack rankings for our clients.

Intent: Users want something nearby, right now.

LCP
Largest Contentful Paint

Loading speed. How long does it take for the main content to load? Target: Under 2.5 seconds

Fix: Defer non‑critical scripts, reduce JavaScript
CLS

Fix: Set image dimensions, avoid layout shifts
