What is Technical SEO?

Technical SEO involves optimizing your website’s infrastructure to make it easy for search engines to crawl, index, and rank your pages effectively. Key areas include speed, mobile-friendliness, structured data, and proper indexing.

  • Website speed & Core Web Vitals
  • Mobile responsiveness & mobile-first indexing
  • Secure HTTPS connection
  • XML sitemap & robots.txt configuration
  • Structured data & schema markup
  • Canonical URLs & duplicate content management
  • Internal linking architecture

Why Technical SEO Matters for Kenyan Businesses

Without proper technical SEO, even high-quality content may fail to rank. Businesses often suffer from:

  • Low Google rankings
  • High bounce rates due to slow or unresponsive sites
  • Poor mobile experience
  • Incomplete indexing and missed traffic opportunities

Step 1: Website Audit & Crawlability

Use tools like Google Search Console, Screaming Frog, Ahrefs, or SEMrush to audit your website. Check for:

  • Broken links (404 errors)
  • Redirect chains & loops
  • Pages blocked by robots.txt or meta tags
  • Indexation status

Step 2: Website Speed & Core Web Vitals

Page speed impacts rankings and user experience. Metrics to monitor:

  • LCP (Largest Contentful Paint) under 2.5s
  • FID (First Input Delay) under 100ms
  • CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift) under 0.1

Optimize images, enable caching, and minimize render-blocking scripts.

Step 3: Mobile-First Indexing

Ensure full mobile responsiveness and fast loading on smartphones. Check readable text, accessible buttons, and proper scaling.

Step 4: HTTPS & Security

Install SSL certificates and redirect HTTP traffic to HTTPS to build trust and improve rankings.

Step 5: XML Sitemap & Robots.txt

Ensure your sitemap is submitted to Google Search Console and robots.txt isn’t blocking important pages. Use canonical URLs to prevent duplicate content.
