2. Your Website Is Not Indexed

If your website is not indexed on Google, it will never rank. Common causes: no sitemap, robots.txt blocking, or never submitted to Search Console.

Fix: Submit your site to Google Search Console, request indexing manually, and use internal linking. Technical SEO audit →

3. Weak Content (Low Quality Pages)

Google ranks websites with high-quality SEO content targeting search intent. Thin content (under 300 words) or irrelevant content won’t rank.

Fix: Create comprehensive content (800+ words). Target long-tail keywords. Include internal links. SEO content writing →

4. No Internal Linking Structure

Without internal links:

  • Google can't crawl your pages
  • Users leave quickly
  • Page authority is lost

Fix: Link to your homepage, service pages, and related blog posts using descriptive anchor text.

5. Technical SEO Problems

Common technical issues:

  • Slow website speed (>3 seconds)
  • Mobile usability problems
  • Broken pages (404 errors)
  • Crawl errors

Fix: Run a technical SEO audit. Compress images, enable caching, fix broken links. Technical SEO guide →

6. No Backlinks (Low Authority)

Backlinks are Google’s trust signals. Without them, your site has low domain authority and won’t rank against competitors.

Fix: Build backlinks from local directories, news sites, and industry blogs. Link building services →

7. Targeting Competitive Keywords

Generic, high‑competition keywords like “SEO” are impossible for small businesses to rank for. Instead, use low competition long-tail SEO keywords for Kenyan businesses.

  • “affordable SEO services for small businesses in Nairobi”
  • “local SEO expert for shops in Kenya”
