1. Your Website Has No SEO Strategy

Many small business websites fail because they don’t target the right keywords. Using generic phrases like “digital marketing” or “SEO” won’t help your site rank locally.

Instead, focus on high-intent, local searches, such as:

  • “best SEO services in Nairobi”
  • “affordable digital marketing agency in Kenya”
  • “Shopify SEO expert Nairobi Kenya”

Fix: Build a clear SEO strategy using long-tail keywords. Learn more in our Best SEO Services in Nairobi Guide.

---

2. Your Website Isn’t Indexed by Google

If Google hasn’t indexed your website, it won’t appear in search results — no matter how great your content is.

Fix:

  • Submit your website via Google Search Console
  • Request indexing for each page
  • Ensure your sitemap.xml is updated
  • Use internal linking to help Google discover pages
---

3. Weak or Thin Content

Google rewards websites with high-quality, helpful, and keyword-optimized content.

Content that is too short, off-topic, or not structured for search intent will fail to rank.

Fix: Publish content targeting long-tail keywords for small businesses in Kenya. For example:

  • “how to optimize website pages for SEO in Kenya”
  • “local SEO strategies for small businesses Nairobi”
  • “Shopify SEO guide for Kenyan stores”

Internal linking to related posts, such as On-Page SEO Checklist for Beginners or Content Marketing & Storytelling, boosts authority and indexing.

---

4. No Internal Linking Structure

Internal links guide both users and Google across your website. Without them:

  • Google struggles to crawl your site
  • Visitors leave too quickly
  • SEO authority is diluted

Fix: Always link to:

  • SEO services
  • case studies
  • contact section
---

5. Poor Technical SEO

Technical issues can completely block your rankings. Common problems include:

  • Slow website speed
  • Mobile responsiveness issues
  • Broken links or 404 errors
  • No HTTPS / insecure site

Fix: Check your site with tools like Google PageSpeed Insights, fix broken links, and ensure mobile responsiveness. Learn more in our Technical SEO Guide.

---

6. No Backlinks (Authority Problem)

Google trusts websites that others link to. If your site has no backlinks, it will struggle to rank.

Fix: Reach out to authoritative sites in your industry, submit guest posts, or collaborate with influencers to gain backlinks.

---

7. Targeting Highly Competitive Keywords

Trying to rank for broad keywords like “SEO” is nearly impossible for small businesses.

Fix: Use long-tail, local keywords such as:

  • “affordable SEO services for small businesses in Nairobi”
  • “local SEO expert for shops in Kenya”
