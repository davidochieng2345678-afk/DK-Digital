📋 What Is Indexing – And Why Does It Matter?

Indexing is the process where Google stores and organises your web pages in its massive database (the search index). When a user searches for something, Google looks through its index – not the live web – to find the most relevant results.

Think of it like a library:

  • Crawling = The librarian walks through the library, looking at every book on the shelves.
  • Indexing = The librarian catalogues each book in the library system so people can find it later.
  • Ranking = The librarian recommends the best books based on what you're looking for.

If Google crawls your site but doesn't index it, your pages never appear in search results – no matter how good they are. This is why "why is my website not indexed on Google Kenya" is one of the most common questions we hear from frustrated business owners.

🏛️ Topic Clusters & Pillar Pages – The Modern Internal Linking Strategy

💡 How it works: The pillar page links to all cluster content, and each cluster piece links back to the pillar page. This sends clear authority signals to Google, boosting the pillar page's rankings – which then lifts all the cluster content.

✅ Link to a specific service page

✅ Pillar page → Cluster content → Sub‑clusters

Too many internal links can look spammy. Aim for 3‑5 internal links per blog post and 5‑7 on service pages.
✅ 4 internal links in a 1,200‑word article
❌ 20+ internal links in a 500‑word article

How to check if you're mobile‑friendly:

  • Google Search Console – The "Mobile Usability" report shows specific issues.
  • Manual test – Open your site on your phone – if it's hard to tap or read, you need fixes.

How to fix it:

  • Use a responsive theme – Most modern themes are responsive by default.
  • Increase tap targets – Buttons and links should be at least 48x48px.
  • Use readable font sizes – Body text should be 16px minimum.
  • Set the viewport – Ensure you have <meta name="viewport" content="width=device-width, initial‑scale=1">.
  • Test on real devices – Emulators are useful, but real phones give the most accurate picture.

💡 Quick win: If you're on WordPress, switch to a mobile‑optimised theme like GeneratePress, Astra, or Kadence. They're built for speed and mobile performance.

How to fix it:

  • Redirect HTTP to HTTPS – Use a 301 redirect to send all traffic to the secure version.
  • Update internal links – Ensure all internal links use HTTPS.
  • Test your SSL – Use SSL Labs' free SSL Test to check your certificate.

  • Add Review schema – Shows star ratings in search results.
  • Add FAQ schema – For blog posts and service pages with Q&As.
