4. Identifying NAP Inconsistencies – What to Look For

NAP inconsistency means your business name, address, or phone number is not identical across directories. Even small differences confuse Google. Here are the most common inconsistencies we find in Kenyan directories:

4.1 Business Name Variations

  • “The Coffee Shop” vs “Coffee Shop” (missing “The”)
  • “Cafe” vs “Café” (accent marks)
  • “ABC Restaurant – Kilimani” vs “ABC Restaurant” (added location suffix)
  • “John’s Plumbing Services” vs “John’s Plumbing” (abbreviation)

Standardise to: Your exact legal business name, no extra words.

4.2 Address Variations

  • “Ngong Road” vs “Ngong Rd”
  • “Kilimani, Nairobi” vs “Nairobi” vs “Nairobi, Kenya”
  • “123 Ngong Road” vs “123 Ngong Road, Kilimani”
  • “Opposite Yaya Centre” vs “Near Yaya Centre”

Standardise to: Use the exact address format from your Google Business Profile (or the format required by the directory).

4.3 Phone Number Variations

  • “0710 123 456” vs “0710123456” vs “0710-123-456”
  • “+254710123456” vs “0710123456”
  • Missing area code “123456”
  • “0710xxxxxx” vs “0700xxxxxx” (wrong number)

Standardise to: 0710123456 (no spaces, no hyphens, no country code). Use this format unless a directory specifically requires spaces or country code.

5. How to Fix Inconsistencies – Step by Step

Once you have identified inconsistent citations, you need to fix them. Here is the process:

  1. Claim the listing – Many directories allow you to claim your business. Look for “Claim this business”, “Edit listing”, or “Suggest an edit”. You may need to verify ownership via phone or email.
  2. Update the NAP fields – Enter your standardised name, address, and phone number exactly as they appear on your Google Business Profile.
  3. Fill in missing fields – Add hours, description, photos, website URL, and attributes (M‑Pesa, free Wi‑Fi, etc.).
  4. Save and wait for approval – Some directories approve changes instantly; others take 1-7 days.
  5. Re‑check after 2 weeks – Ensure the changes stuck. Sometimes directories revert changes.

If you cannot claim the listing: Use the “Suggest an edit” feature. Most directories allow users to suggest corrections. Google Maps has a “Suggest an edit” button on every listing.

6. Finding and Removing Duplicate Listings

Duplicates are especially common on Google Business Profile, Yellow Pages, and Cybo. Signs of duplicates:

  • Two listings for the same business with slightly different names or addresses.
  • A listing from an old address (e.g., if you moved years ago).
  • A listing created by a previous owner or by a third‑party service.

How to remove duplicate GBP listings:

  1. Log into Google Business Profile.
  2. If you see duplicate listings, click on the duplicate.
  3. Go to “Info” → “Close or remove this listing” → “Remove listing”.
  4. Wait 1-2 weeks for Google to process.

For other directories: Use the “Claim this business” feature for the primary listing. For duplicates, look for a “Report a duplicate” or “Merge listings” option. If not available, contact the directory’s support.

1. Why NAP Consistency Matters So Much – Google’s Trust Signal

Google’s local algorithm works like a detective. It crawls the web, finds mentions of your business, and compares them. When it sees the same name, address, and phone number across many trusted directories, it concludes: “This business is real, legitimate, and trustworthy.”

When it sees conflicting information – for example, “The Coffee Shop” on one directory and “Coffee Shop” on another – it gets confused. It may:

  • Downgrade your Local Pack ranking (or remove you entirely).
  • Show the wrong phone number to customers (leading to missed calls).
  • Show the wrong address (leading to frustrated customers).
  • Create duplicate listings that compete against each other.

2. Create Your Master NAP Document (Single Source of Truth)

Before you fix any inconsistencies, you must decide on a single, canonical version of your NAP. This is your “source of truth”. Every directory and citation must match this exactly – character for character.

2.1 Business Name – Standardisation Rules

  • Use your legal business name as registered. Not a shortened version, not a “trading as” name unless that is what customers search for.
  • Do not add location suffixes (e.g., “ABC Restaurant – Kilimani”). The address field handles location.
  • Do not add keywords (e.g., “Best Coffee Shop Nairobi”). This violates Google’s guidelines.
  • Decide on abbreviations: “Cafe” vs “Café” – pick one. “&” vs “and” – pick one. “Ltd” vs “Limited” – pick one.
  • Remove unnecessary punctuation. “John’s Plumbing” not “John’s Plumbing!”

Example (café): “The Coffee Shop” (not “Coffee Shop”, “The Coffee Shop – Kilimani”, or “T.C.S.”).

2.2 Address – Standardisation Rules

  • Use the exact address format from your Google Business Profile. This is the most important directory.
  • Spell out street names or abbreviate consistently. “Ngong Road” or “Ngong Rd” – pick one.
  • Include the estate/suburb if relevant. “123 Ngong Road, Kilimani, Nairobi” – not just “123 Ngong Road, Nairobi”.
  • Landmarks are optional. If you use a landmark, keep it consistent: “Opposite Yaya Centre” not “Near Yaya Centre” on some directories.
  • Do not include PO Box as your main address. Google requires a physical address. You can add PO Box separately if a directory allows.
  • Use consistent country format. “Kenya” not “KE” or “Republic of Kenya”.

Example (café): “123 Ngong Road, Kilimani, Nairobi, Kenya” (no “Opposite Yaya Centre” – they added that in the description instead).

2.3 Phone Number – Standardisation Rules

  • Use the Kenyan local format: 0710123456 (no spaces, no hyphens, no country code). This is the most common format on Kenyan directories.
  • If a directory requires international format: Use +254710123456 (still no spaces or hyphens). But try to use local format first.
  • Never include “0710 123 456” or “0710-123-456” as your primary format – these variations will cause inconsistency.
  • Use the same phone number for all directories. Do not use a different number for different directories.

Example (café): “0710123456” (local format) is their master. They use “+254710123456” only when a directory explicitly rejects local format.

2.4 Website URL – Standardisation Rules

  • Use the full URL: https://www.yourdomain.com (not “yourdomain.com” or “http://yourdomain.com”).
  • Add UTM parameters to track which directory sends traffic. Example: `https://www.yourdomain.com/?utm_source=yellowpages&utm_medium=directory&utm_campaign=citations`.
  • Use the same URL across all directories. Do not use different landing pages for different directories.

7. Review Schema Markup – Display Stars in Search Results

Review schema (aka “AggregateRating” schema) adds star ratings to your search results. This increases click‑through rate (CTR) by 30‑50%.

7.1 How to Add Review Schema (JSON‑LD)

Add this code to the `` or before `` of your homepage or products page. Replace the example values with your actual review count and rating.

<script type="application/ld+json">
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "LocalBusiness",
  "name": "The Coffee Shop",
  "aggregateRating": {
    "@type": "AggregateRating",
    "ratingValue": "4.9",
    "reviewCount": "47"
  }
}
</script>

7.2 For Product Pages (E‑commerce)

<script type="application/ld+json">
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",

  }
}
</script>

❓ Frequently Asked Questions – Local Citations

What are local citations?
Local citations are mentions of your business NAP (Name, Address, Phone) on other websites – directories, review sites, blogs. They boost local SEO and Google Maps rankings.
How long do citations take to work?
Typically 1–3 months to see ranking improvements, depending on competition and citation quality. Some changes (like fixing NAP) can show impact in weeks.
Do I need to pay for citations?
Many directories are free. Paid citations offer additional visibility but are not required. We focus on high‑quality free directories first.
How many citations do I need?
Quality over quantity. 20–30 consistent, high-authority citations are better than 100 low-quality ones. NAP consistency is more important than count.
What is NAP consistency and why does it matter?
NAP stands for Name, Address, Phone. Google checks that your business info is consistent across all directories. Inconsistent NAP confuses Google and hurts your local rankings.
