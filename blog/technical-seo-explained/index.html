Core Technical SEO Elements – 9 Key Areas

1. Website Crawling & Indexing

Search engines use bots to crawl and index your pages. If your site isn’t crawlable, Google can’t rank it.

Check for: Robots.txt errors, XML sitemap issues, broken or redirecting links.

Fix: Update your sitemap, fix broken links, and ensure no important pages are blocked.

2. Website Speed Optimization

Slow websites lose visitors and rankings. Speed is a confirmed ranking factor.

Fix: Compress images, minify CSS/JS, use caching and CDN solutions.

For step‑by‑step speed optimization, see our On-Page SEO Checklist.

3. Mobile‑Friendliness

Most users in Kenya browse on mobile. Google prioritizes mobile‑friendly sites.

Fix: Make your site responsive, fast‑loading on mobile, and easy to navigate. Mobile SEO services →

4. HTTPS & Secure Website

Security matters for SEO. Google favors HTTPS websites.

Fix: Install SSL certificates and redirect HTTP pages to HTTPS.

5. URL Structure

Clean URLs improve user experience and SEO.

Fix: Keep URLs short, include relevant keywords, avoid unnecessary parameters. Example: /blog/technical-seo-explained-2026.html

6. Fix Broken Links (404 Errors)

Broken links reduce crawlability and frustrate users.

Fix: Regularly check your website for 404 errors and fix or redirect them using 301 redirects.

7. Structured Data (Schema Markup)

Schema markup helps Google understand your content for rich results, which improves CTR and visibility.

Fix: Use BlogPosting schema for blogs, Product schema for e‑commerce, LocalBusiness schema for local SEO.

8. Internal Linking

Internal linking distributes SEO power, helps Google crawl your site, and guides visitors.

Fix: Link to your services, case studies, and contact section using descriptive anchor text.

9. Duplicate Content Issues

Duplicate content confuses Google and can reduce rankings.

Fix: Use canonical tags and ensure all content is unique.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions – Technical SEO

What is technical SEO?
Technical SEO optimizes your website structure so search engines can crawl, index, and understand your content – essential for rankings. It includes speed, mobile, indexing, and structured data.
How long does technical SEO take to show results?
Some fixes (like broken links, meta tags) can show impact in days. Full site optimization typically takes 1–3 months.
Why am I not ranking on Google?
Likely due to technical issues (speed, indexing, broken links) or weak SEO strategy. A free audit can identify the exact cause.

What is Core Web Vitals?
Core Web Vitals are Google’s metrics for loading speed (LCP), interactivity (FID), and visual stability (CLS). Poor scores hurt rankings.
How do I check if my site is mobile‑friendly?
Use Google’s Mobile‑Friendly Test tool. If it fails, your site needs responsive design fixes.
