📌 Technical SEO Defined

Technical SEO is the process of optimizing your website's infrastructure so that search engines can crawl, index, and rank your pages effectively. Unlike content SEO or off-page SEO, technical SEO focuses on how your website is built — not what's written on it.

Think of technical SEO as the foundation of a house. No matter how beautiful the interior (content) or how many visitors come (traffic), if the foundation is cracked, the whole structure is at risk. Technical SEO ensures your website's foundation is solid so everything else can perform at its best.

Key components of technical SEO include:

  • Site Speed & Performance — how fast your pages load
  • Mobile-Friendliness — how well your site works on mobile devices
  • Crawlability — how easily search engines can discover your content
  • Indexability — whether search engines can store and show your pages
  • Site Architecture — how your pages are structured and linked
  • Structured Data — code that helps search engines understand your content
  • Security — HTTPS and SSL implementation
  • URL Structure — clean, descriptive, and SEO-friendly URLs
  • Canonical Tags — preventing duplicate content issues

Why speed matters for rankings, user experience, and conversions — and how to fix your site's performance issues

📌 Core Web Vitals — What They Are and Why They Matter

Core Web Vitals are Google's metrics for measuring user experience. They evaluate three key aspects of your website's performance: loading speed, interactivity, and visual stability. Since 2021, Core Web Vitals have been official ranking factors for Google.

Think of Core Web Vitals as a report card for your website's performance. If you fail, Google ranks you lower. If you pass, you get a ranking boost. It's that simple.

Keywords: "Core Web Vitals Kenya," "site speed ranking factor Nairobi," "Google performance metrics Mombasa," "web vitals optimization Kisumu"

📊 The Three Core Web Vitals — Explained

🖼️

LCP — Largest Contentful Paint

What it measures: The time it takes for the largest content element on your page to load — typically an image, video, or large text block.

Why it matters: LCP tells you how quickly your page "appears" to users. A fast LCP means users see content quickly and don't abandon your site.

Google's requirement: Good: under 2.5 seconds · Needs Improvement: 2.5-4.0 seconds · Poor: over 4.0 seconds

How to fix:
• Optimize and compress images
• Implement lazy loading
• Remove render-blocking resources
• Use a CDN (Content Delivery Network)
• Upgrade hosting

Keywords: "LCP optimization Kenya," "largest contentful paint Nairobi," "page load speed Mombasa"

👆

FID — First Input Delay

What it measures: The time it takes for your page to respond to a user's first interaction — clicking a link, tapping a button, or typing in a form.

Why it matters: A fast FID means your page is responsive. Users can interact with your site immediately without frustrating delays.

Google's requirement: Good: under 100 milliseconds · Needs Improvement: 100-300 milliseconds · Poor: over 300 milliseconds

How to fix:
• Minimize JavaScript execution time
• Break up long tasks
• Use web workers
• Optimize third-party scripts
• Reduce main thread work

Keywords: "FID optimization Kenya," "first input delay Nairobi," "interactivity speed Mombasa"

📐

CLS — Cumulative Layout Shift

What it measures: The visual stability of your page — how much elements move around as the page loads.

Why it matters: A low CLS means your page is stable. Users don't accidentally click the wrong button because content shifted unexpectedly.

Google's requirement: Good: under 0.1 · Needs Improvement: 0.1-0.25 · Poor: over 0.25

How to fix:
• Set dimensions for images and videos
• Reserve space for ads and embeds
• Use aspect-ratio CSS
• Avoid inserting new content above existing content
• Use font-display CSS to prevent layout shifts

Keywords: "CLS optimization Kenya," "cumulative layout shift Nairobi," "visual stability Mombasa"

💡 The Bottom Line: Passing Core Web Vitals is essential for ranking, user experience, and conversions. A poor score can cost you up to 50% of your traffic.
