🔗 [What's the difference between off-page SEO and on-page SEO?]

On-page SEO focuses on your website itself — content, meta tags, structure, keywords, user experience, and technical optimization. It's what you can control directly on your site.

Off-page SEO focuses on building authority and trust through external signals — backlinks, citations, brand mentions, social signals, and digital PR. It's what Google uses to determine if your site is trustworthy and valuable.

Both are essential. On-page SEO makes your site rankable; off-page SEO makes it rank. Without off-page SEO, your on-page efforts will never reach their full potential.

🤔 [What's the difference between guest posting and niche edits?]

Guest posting involves creating new, original content for another website. You write a complete article, which is published on the target site with a backlink to your site. This builds authority, drives referral traffic, and increases brand awareness.

Niche edits involve inserting your link into an existing article that's already published and ranking. The content is already there — we just add your link contextually. This gives you the authority of an established page without the need for new content creation.

Which is better? Both are valuable. Guest posting builds fresh authority and brand awareness; niche edits give you quick authority from established pages. We use both strategies in our packages for maximum results.
