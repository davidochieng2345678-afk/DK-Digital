📊 The 3 Local Pack Ranking Factors – Proximity, Relevance, Prominence

Google determines which businesses appear in the Local Pack based on three key factors: Proximity, Relevance, and Prominence. Here's how each one works:

📍
Proximity

How close is your business to the searcher? Google uses the user's location to show the nearest relevant businesses. If you're far away, you won't appear – no matter how good your profile is.

💡 Fix: Ensure your GBP address is accurate and your service area is set correctly.
🎯
Relevance

How well does your business match what the user is searching for? Google looks at your categories, attributes, description, and keywords to determine if you're relevant to the search query.

💡 Fix: Choose accurate primary and secondary categories, and include location keywords in your description.
⭐
Prominence

How well‑known and trusted is your business? Google uses reviews, ratings, backlinks, citations, and overall online presence to determine prominence. More reviews = more prominence.

💡 Fix: Generate more positive reviews, build local citations, and maintain NAP consistency.

💡 The secret: Most Kenyan businesses focus on one factor and ignore the others. To rank in the Local Pack, you need all three. Proximity gets you in the running, relevance gets you considered, and prominence gets you to #1.

🏆 How the Local Pack Algorithm Works – A Simple Breakdown

Here's a simplified version of how Google decides which 3 businesses to show in the Local Pack:

1
Step 1: Filter by Proximity – Google identifies businesses within a certain radius of the searcher's location. If you're too far away, you're automatically excluded.
2
Step 2: Rank by Relevance – Google scores each business on how well it matches the search query. Your categories, attributes, and description content matter here.
3
Step 3: Sort by Prominence – Google ranks the relevant businesses by prominence. Businesses with more reviews, higher ratings, and stronger citations rank higher.
4
Step 4: Display the Top 3 – Google shows the top 3 scoring businesses in the Local Pack, with the highest scorer at #1.
5
Step 5: Show "See More" – If the user clicks "See More," Google shows additional results beyond the top 3.

✅ The strategy: To get into the Local Pack, you need to optimise all three factors – proximity (accurate address), relevance (right categories, description), and prominence (reviews, citations). Most businesses focus on one and miss the others.
