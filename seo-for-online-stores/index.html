🛍️ [What's the difference between e-commerce SEO and regular SEO?]

Regular SEO focuses on improving a website's overall visibility — blog posts, service pages, and informational content. It targets a broad range of keywords and audiences.

E-commerce SEO is specialized for online stores. It focuses on product page optimization, category SEO, buyer-intent keywords, schema markup (Product schema), internal linking between products, and conversion optimization. It's designed to drive sales, not just traffic.

Think of it this way: Regular SEO gets people to your site. E-commerce SEO gets them to buy once they're there — and keeps them coming back.

🧩 [What is structured data (schema) and why do I need it?]

Structured data (schema markup) is code you add to your product pages to help search engines understand your products better. It creates rich snippets — enhanced search results with product ratings, pricing, availability, and reviews.

Why you need it: Rich snippets stand out in search results and increase click-through rates by 30-40%. For product pages, the right schema (Product, Offer, Review, AggregateRating) can display:

  • • Price and availability — shows if the product is in stock
  • • Ratings and reviews — displays star ratings from customers
  • • Product images — shows product photos directly in search results
  • • Product descriptions — gives shoppers a preview of what you sell

We implement the right schema types for your store to maximize visibility and clicks. It's included in all our monthly packages.

Over the years, I've seen stores lose out on thousands of customers because of these avoidable errors. Here are the most common e-commerce SEO mistakes and how to fix them.

❌

[Thin or Duplicate Product Descriptions]

Mistake: Using short (under 200 words) or copied supplier descriptions. Fix: Expand descriptions to 500+ words and make them unique. Include features, benefits, and buyer-intent keywords. This eliminates duplicate content penalties and improves rankings.

❌

[Ignoring Site Speed]

Mistake: Having a slow-loading store (over 3 seconds). Fix: Optimize images, enable caching, and remove unnecessary apps. Every 1-second improvement can increase conversions by 7%. A faster store ranks higher and sells more.

[Targeting Generic Keywords Only]

Mistake: Optimizing for broad terms like "shoes" instead of "buy running shoes Kenya." Fix: Prioritize buyer-intent keywords that include "buy," "order," "price," or location. These attract shoppers ready to purchase.

❌

[No Internal Linking Structure]

Mistake: Products exist in isolation with no links between them. Fix: Build a silo structure — category pages as pillars, linking to sub-categories and products. Add cross-sell and related product links on every product page.

[Not Using Schema Markup]
