Why E‑Commerce SEO Is Different – Unique Challenges

E‑commerce SEO is not the same as standard SEO. Online stores face challenges that content websites and service businesses don't. Here are the key differences:

Duplicate Product Descriptions

Manufacturers often provide the same description for the same product across multiple stores. This creates duplicate content that confuses Google and hurts rankings.

Faceted Navigation

Filters (size, color, price) create infinite URL combinations that can waste Google's crawl budget and cause duplicate content issues.

Large Image Libraries

Hundreds or thousands of images can slow down your site and hurt Core Web Vitals – a key ranking factor for e‑commerce.

Complex Site Architecture

Online stores often have deep, complex site structures – home → categories → sub‑categories → products – making it hard for Google to find and rank all pages.

Limited Keyword Opportunities

Product pages often target long‑tail, specific keywords with limited search volume. Category pages target broader, more competitive terms.

Mobile‑First Shopping

70%+ of Kenyan e‑commerce traffic comes from mobile devices. If your store isn't perfectly mobile‑optimized, you're losing both rankings and sales.

Our approach: We address each of these challenges with a platform‑specific, data‑driven strategy – ensuring your online store is fully optimized for both search engines and buyers.

Product Page Optimization – The Complete Checklist

Your product pages are where customers make buying decisions. They also need to be fully optimized for search engines. Here's our complete product page optimization checklist:

Title Tags & Meta Descriptions

We craft unique, keyword‑rich titles (50‑60 characters) and compelling meta descriptions (150‑160 characters) that include product name, brand, and primary keyword. Titles should include the product name + category + brand + location if relevant – e.g., "Nike Air Max 270 – Buy Running Shoes Online Nairobi | TechElectro."

Unique Product Descriptions

We never use manufacturer descriptions. We write unique, persuasive, keyword‑optimized descriptions that highlight features, benefits, and use cases. Descriptions include the primary keyword naturally, plus secondary keywords like "durable," "affordable," "best price in Kenya," etc. We also add E‑E‑A‑T signals – sourcing information, quality certifications, and expert reviews.

Image Optimization

We compress images to reduce file size without losing quality, add descriptive alt text with keywords (e.g., "Nike Air Max 270 men's running shoes Nairobi"), and use descriptive file names (nike-air-max-270-kenya.jpg). We also implement lazy loading to improve page speed.

URL Structure & Internal Linking

We create clean, descriptive URLs – /products/nike-air-max-270 – not /products?id=12345. We also build a strategic internal linking network that connects related products, categories, and blog posts to distribute link equity and improve navigation.

Schema Markup (Structured Data)

We implement Product schema, Review schema, and Breadcrumb schema using JSON‑LD. This enables rich snippets – star ratings, price, availability, and reviews – which increase click‑through rates by up to 30% and help Google understand your products better.

Mobile‑First Optimization

We ensure product pages are fully responsive on mobile devices – buttons are tappable, text is readable, images load quickly, and the checkout process is seamless. Mobile‑first indexing means Google primarily uses the mobile version of your site for ranking.

Client example: A fashion store with 300 products saw 45 product pages reach page 1 within 60 days after we rewrote all descriptions, optimized titles, and added schema markup. Revenue doubled in 6 months.

Category & Collection SEO – Ranking for Broader Keywords

Category pages are often the highest‑traffic pages on an e‑commerce site. They target broader, higher‑volume keywords (e.g., "running shoes," "women's dresses," "electronics") that bring in top‑of‑funnel traffic. Here's how we optimize them:

Unique Category Descriptions

We write unique, keyword‑rich descriptions for every category and collection page – not just a list of products. These descriptions include the target keyword, sub‑keywords, and a clear value proposition for customers. We also add sub‑category links and buyer guides to make the page more valuable.

Title & Meta Optimization

Category titles include the primary keyword + location + brand – e.g., "Running Shoes Kenya – Buy Best Running Shoes Online | TechElectro." Meta descriptions highlight the range of products, prices, and unique selling points.

Internal Linking Structure

We build a hierarchical internal linking structure – home → main categories → sub‑categories → products. Each category links to its sub‑categories and top products, passing link equity and helping Google understand your site's architecture.

Category Schema Markup

We add Breadcrumb and Category schema to help Google understand your category hierarchy and display breadcrumbs in search results – improving CTR and user experience.

Technical E‑Commerce Fixes – The Essentials

E‑commerce stores often suffer from technical issues that cripple SEO performance. Here are the critical fixes we implement:

Faceted Navigation & URL Parameters

We configure canonical tags and robots.txt to prevent indexation of filter URLs (e.g., /products?color=red&size=10). We also implement rel="nofollow" on faceted links and use Google Search Console's URL parameters tool to tell Google how to handle them.

Duplicate Content Fixes

We add canonical tags to consolidate duplicate pages (e.g., product variants, print view, sort orders). We also rewrite thin or duplicate descriptions to make each page unique and valuable.

Site Speed & Core Web Vitals

We optimize LCP, FID, and CLS – compressing images, minifying CSS/JS, using CDN, enabling caching, and reducing server response time. We also remove slow apps and plugins that bloat your store.

XML Sitemap & Robots.txt

We create dynamic XML sitemaps that include all product, category, and blog pages – and update them automatically. We also configure robots.txt to block crawl of irrelevant pages (cart, checkout, internal search) and save crawl budget.

Mobile‑First & AMP

We ensure your store is fully responsive and passes Google's mobile‑friendly test. For large stores, we implement AMP (Accelerated Mobile Pages) for product pages to boost mobile speed and rankings.

404 & Redirect Management

We identify and fix broken links and 404 errors – redirecting them to relevant products or categories using 301 redirects. This preserves link equity and improves user experience.
